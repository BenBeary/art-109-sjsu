Artist Statment

According to the National Parks Service, human-caused wildfires account for around 85 percent of all wildfires in the United States. California alone has had a median of around 8,265 wildfires a year since the start of the 2000s.

While doing research for this project we found that many of the most destructive fires caused by people tend to be the result of powerlines snapping and not being properly maintained. However, we decided to focus more on fires caused by individuals and less on the malpractice of PG&E in California. We came to this decision because we wanted to focus on fires that would be preventable for an individual and not on fires caused by the mismanagement of corporations. Another similarity between all the fires we researched was the presence of dry grass and how often it seemed to present itself as the fuel that allowed these fires to go out of control. We concluded that looking for ways to get rid of the dry brush would help neutralize the uncanny amount of brush fires in California.

This website brings to light just how easy it is to create some very destructive fires if precaution is not taken into account. The interactive nature of our site allows the viewer to become a participant in a historical event and even stop the event from occurring altogether. The constraint of only being allowed to use HTML code to make the site was frustrating for us to work with in the beginning but after a while, we got the hang of it. The constraint allowed us to focus more on what we wanted to make and the message we wanted to express to a viewer of our site instead of worrying as much about bombarding them with esthetics and distractions.
