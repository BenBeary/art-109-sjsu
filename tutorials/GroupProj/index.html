Your adventure starts here

You and your friends wake up from your tents after drinking around a campfire the night before. You are out in the wilderness hoping to gain some bonding time with people you haven't been able to meet up with in a while. After a quick breakfast, you are ready to head out to go hunting and make some new experiences.

Head out and start hunting with your buddies

You and your friends pack up and begin to travel further into the forest in hopes of finding some bigger game to score.

It takes hours before you see your first herd of deer. You and your friends successfully take down the dear and are able to hog tie to a stick you found on the ground.

Head back to the campsite

Heading back to the campsite you and your friends tell stories about the animal you just hunted and have a laugh with each other on how each of you reacting to the situation at hand. After about an hour of traveling back you start to smell smoke and look to the skies to see nothing but a dark haze blanketing the sky.

uh oh...

You and your buddies drop the dear, completely forgeting about it, and rush to the treeline into a small meadow of grass and flowers. You look and see a plumes of smoke rising with hints of yellow flames pushing just above the trees. What you feared seams to be true as the raging fire appears to be in the same direction of where your campsite is.

You and your friends race through the forest towards your cars that were parked around 2 miles away from the campsite. by the time you make it to your cars you can start to see the fire in the treeline making its way towards you at an alarming rate. You and your friends speed off back to civilization in the hopes that no one links you to the raging fire that is rapidly expanding behind you.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Try to score another animal

You manage to score a rabbit this time. Not as impressive as the dear, but still a good hunt nevertheless.

After adding the rabbit to the stick that was carrying the dear, you and your friends begin your travel back towards the campsite. On your way back, you start to smell smoke and look to the sky to see that the once clear blue skies blanketed in a dark grey haze of smoke and ash. Your group drop your hunted animals and rush to a nearby meadow to see a raging fire that has engulfed the area around where your campsite used to be.

Run for the Cars!

One of your friends pitch in to say that the fire seems to already be heading towards where you had parked your cars and that there would be no time to make it before being scorched.


Try to find a nearby road

You and your team manage to find a country road nearby and start walking along it away from the fire. Not much later a police cruiser helping with evacuations spots your group and drives you off to the nearest evacuation zone. Once arriving you and your group stick together and are later greeted by two police officers who want to now more about why your group was walking on foot near the giant wildfire that was expanding at an alarming rate. You manage to skirt around the fact that it was started near your campsite, but continued on with your life holding on to the guilt.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Check the campfire

The campfire steams to still have some smoke trailing from it along with a few embers that have a faint red glow.

Douse with water

You take your water bottle and dump it onto the fire causing the smoke trail to stop entirely and any faint red glow to disapear. With the campfire taken care of, you and your group continue onwards and hunt for the rest of the day. The hunt ended late in the afternoon with nothing but good memories with your friends to show for it.
Returning back to the city, You and your friends decide what to do next.

Head to the bar

After a few drinks, one of your friends gets the amazing idea to light off some fire works to celebrate the reunion and try to make the occasion more memorable than a failed hunting trip and a quick chat.

Decline

The idea of fireworks during this time of the year which has been known for large wild fires scares you. You talk them out of it and convince them to just stay at the bar and enjoy the day without it going off with a bang.

-------------------------------------------------------------



I like fireworks

You take your car out to a nearby neighborhood and wait till dusk becomes night so you have the best view of the fireworks. Your friend failed to mention that the fireworks he was talking about were mortar-grade fireworks but it was too late now to stop him as the fuze was already lit.

A Loud explosion

The night sky erupts with a ball of color as the firework expanded rapidly outward with little signs of stopping. This mesmorizing ball turned into a nightmare as multiple embers did not disappear back into the night sky as they usually do. Some end up landing in a near by field which then got illuminated by a growing fire.

Thats not good...

Realising the implications of launching a firework that started a brush fire along with other smaller fires starting to pop up, your group leaves rapidly from the scene as the small brush fires rapidly combine to create one mega fire that is showing no signs of stopping.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Party back at your house

Back at your house, you and your friends watch sports on the couch will drinking beers and reminiscing about the hunt you just came back from. One of your friends brings up that it would be fun to end this meet up with a bang as they brought fireworks.

Not at my house

Shooting fireworks near your home does not feel like the best idea and decline the offer. You finish the day off with some card games and say goodbye to your friends as they head back home.


I do love fireworks

You and your friends move to your backyard where you pyromaniac of a friend places down a mortar tube and places a mortar-grade firework with and lights the fuze. Seconds later the firework wizes into the air and then bursts into a gold, shimmering ball of light.

The ball of light, that should have dwindled out by now, has not and is now rapidly heading towards the ground. You start to panic and call 911 but it is too late as the firework has already started multiple small fires that are rapidly increasing in size. You and your friends try to put some of them out, but there is too many to deal with and decide its best to leave before the flames get strong enough to fight back.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Say your goodbyes and head back home alone

After a long day of hiking through the forest, you decide that you are done for the day. You say your goodbyes to your friends and hope to see them soon before starting the drive back to your home to rest and relax. Although your day was uneventful, it was better than having a bad day.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Wait for fire to cool

You decide to wait for the fire to cool down just in case and let you friends go on without you. After a couple of hours, the smoke from the campfire has stopped and the embers are nothing more than charcoal and ash. You have a faint memory of where your friends said they were going.

Stay put till they get back

You decide it is better not to test your memory and wait it out until your friends get back. Hours later, your friends return with a dear hog tied to a branch and smiles on their faces. They tell you stories of their travel along with the failures of how they missed the first shot. It seemed like a lot of fun and you are sad that you ended up missing out but glad everyone had fun in the end.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Go out and try to find them

You decide that waiting around for your friends to come back would be boring and don't want to be left out of all the fun so you decide to start heading in the direction you saw them leave and try to follow the lightly pressed foot prints in the ground.

After an hour of tracking, the foot steps become to faint to recognize anymore. You are deep within the forest now with no real clue of where they went to next.

Did they go left?

You move further into the forest losing sight of any footprints as the forest floor fills in with dense grasses and plants. After a while you find yourself completely lost within a tall grass meadow with zero clue to where your friends might be. Any form of clue to help you get back is long gone and you start to panic. After screaming for help for a couple of minutes, you try to think of a way to get out of this mess. One thing that pops into your head is creating a smoke signal out of the surrounding brush in the hopes that someone will see it.

Light a fire

After gathering enough twigs, leaves, and branches, you start a small fire and continue. As you throw in some more leaves to try and increase the amount of smoke from the fire, the wind picks up causing the fire to gain a lot of oxygen making the flames rapidly grow to unsustainable heights. The surrounding grass bed catches multiple flying embers in the process and begin to ignite into monstorous flames. You run back into the forrest to escape the fire.

Hours later, a helicopter responding to the massive fire you unintentially started spots you and rescues you. Although this was not what you were thinking about when making a signal fire, it did end up getting you out of being stranded.


Clear an area

After spending some time digging a small hole and clearing an area around it from anything flammable, you finally manage to set up a fire that could produce a decent amount of smoke. Winds started to pick up later on but the fire was low enough into the ground to not be affected by it. Hours later your friends end up finding you. You are both happy to have found each other as you start walking back to your campsite to talk about this terrifying experience.

-------------------------------------------------------------



Head back to camp

Deciding it's not worth it to get lost in a forest you don't know too well was probably a good idea. You travel back to the campsite using the same foot prints from earlier and wait for your friends to return back. Hours later, your friends return with a dear hog tied to a branch and smiles on their faces. They tell you stories of their travel along with the failures of how they missed the first shot. It seemed like a lot of fun and you are sad that you ended up missing out but glad everyone had fun in the end.
